Investigation Overview¶

In this presentation, I will be investigating a dataset related to Ford GoBike. The goal of this investigation is to analyze the trip duration using several variables.

Dataset Overview¶

The Ford GoBike trip dataset is from 2019.

It contains ride information about people using Ford GoBike. These information include the time when the ride has started and ended, places (including longtitude & latitude) where the ride has started and ended, the duration of the ride, etc... In addition to that, there are some information about the users like their gender, birth year, and their type (whether they are subscribers or customers).

The original dataset consists of 183412 rows and 16 columns.

There were some modifications in the dataset that were perfomed. These modifications include removing irrelevant/inaccurate parts from the datset and changing data types of some labels. To prepare the dataset for analysis, it was cleansed as follows:

  • Remove bike trips that do not have start_station_id, start_station_name, end_station_id & end_station_name

  • Remove bike trips whose memebers do not have member_birth_year & member_gender

  • Convert column types of start_time & end_time from string to date time

Dataset Overview¶

I also did additional transformations throughout the exploratory analysis, which are:

  • Converting duration_sec to duration_min
  • Removing all trips that lasted longer than 300 mins (outliers)
  • Removing all trips whose users are older than 76 (outliers)
  • Removing all trips whose users' gender is not identified (not useful)
  • Adding a new column start_hour which indicates the hour at which the trip started
  • Adding a new column day which indicates whether the trip took place on a weekday or a weekend
  • Adding a new column member_age which indicates the user's age

Start Hour Count¶

This plot shows the trip count of each hour throughout the day. It is a bimodal distrbution. The peaks are at 8:00 o'clock in the morning and 17:00 o'clock in the evening.

Day of the Week Count vs User Type¶

The next plot shows day of the week versus user type. Users (especially subscribers) tend to use GoBike more during week days. Taking the findings of the previous plot for the start hour also into consideration, we can assume that members use Ford GoBike as a ride to their school/college/work and back home.

User Type vs Trip Duration¶

This plot shows user type versus trip duration. The viloin plot for customers is wider than the one for subscribers. Also, susbcibers' trips on average last less than customers' trips. As stated in the previous part, one can say that subscribers use GoBike as a quick and easy mode of transport to their destination for their daily activities. Also, I assume that subscribers are used to riding bikes and that's why they might be cycling faster.

Day of the Week vs User Type vs Trip Duration¶

The final plot shows the relationship between the day of the week, the user type and the duration. The day of the week and the user type strenghten each other and can indicate the duration of the trip, which is the feature of intereset. Subscribers' rides durations are slightly longer on weekends (not that significant though). Customers' rides last longer than suscribers' rides. Also, customers ride longer during weekends.

Conclusion¶

In conclusion, we have observed that customers use GoBike for longer durations on weekends which makes sense if they use it for wandering around the city. Generally, subscribers use GoBike for shorter durations.
We have also found that the peaks for using GoBike are at 8:00 o'clock in the morning and 17:00 o'clock in the evening and that subscribers use GoBike more on weekdays. This might indicate that they use GoBike as a transportation to/from school/college/work.

I would suggest to make offers for subscribers on weekdays. For customers, applying offers on weekends would be more suitable.
